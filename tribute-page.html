Dr. Muhammad Yunus

A Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, banker, economist, and civil society leader who was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for founding the Grameen Bank and pioneering the concepts of microcredit and microfinance. These loans are given to entrepreneurs too poor to qualify for traditional bank loans.

Nobel Peace Prize Laureate, Yunus Centre Bangladesh

Here's a timeline of Dr. Yunus' life :

  • 1940 - Professor Muhammad Yunus was born.
  • 1944 - his family moved to the city of Chittagong, and he studied at Lamabazar Primary School.
  • 1952 - During his school years, he was an active Boy Scout, and travelled to West Pakistan and India.
  • 1955 - Travelled to Europe, USA and Canada.
  • 1957 - He enrolled in the Department of Economics at Dhaka University.
  • 1959 - Travelled to Philippines and Japan to attend Jamborees .
  • 1960 - Completed his BA.
  • 1961 - Completed his MA. Following his graduation, Prof Yunus joined the Bureau of Economics, Dhaka University. Later he was appointed as a lecturer in economics in Chittagong College.
  • 1965 - He was offered a Fulbright scholarship to study in the United States.
  • 1969 - He obtained his PhD in economics from Vanderbilt University in the US.
  • 1971, During The Liberation war of Bangladesh - Prof Yunus founded a Citizen’s Committee in Nashville, TN, published a newsletter named Bangladesh Newsletter, and ran the Bangladesh Information Center in Washington DC with other Bangladeshis living in the US, to raise support for the liberation of East Pakistan and lobby at the US Congress to stop military aid to Pakistan.
  • 1969 - 1972 - He was an assistant professor of economics at Middle Tennessee State University in Murfreesboro, TN.
  • 1972 - Prof Yunus returned to the country and joined the Economics Department of University of Chittagong after a brief spell in the Planning Commission.
  • 1974 - He became actively involved with poverty reduction after observing the famine and established the Rural Economics Programme as part of the department’s academic programme.
  • 1975 - He organised Nabajug (New Era) Tebhaga Khamar (three share farm), which the government later adopted as the Packaged Input Programme.
  • 1976 - During visits to very poor households in the village of Jobra near Chittagong University, Prof Yunus discovered that very small loans could make an enormous difference to a poor person’s life. Prof Yunus strongly believed that, given the chance, the poor would repay the borrowed money, and that it would help them work their way out of poverty. After many efforts, he finally succeeded in securing a credit line from Janata Bank, offering himself as the guarantor, for his project to lend to the poor in Jobra
  • 1978 - He received the President’s Award.
  • 1983 - The project was converted into a fully fledged bank named Grameen Bank (Village Bank), specialising in making small loans to the poor.
  • 1985 - He received the Central Bank Award.
  • 1987 - He received the Indipendence Award (Highest National Award).
  • 2006 - Muhammad Yunus was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize, along with Grameen Bank, for their efforts to create economic and social development.
  • As of May, 2008 - Grameen Bank (GB) has 7.5-million borrowers, 97% of whom are women. With 2 515 branches, GB provides services in 82 072 villages, covering more than 97% of the villages in Bangladesh. It has lent over $7-billion to poor people since its inception and the repayment rate has been near 100%. All its money comes from the depositors of the bank.
